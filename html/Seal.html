Monk Seal

Monk Seal Facts

  • Length: 2.1–2.4 m (7–8 ft)
  • Weight: 170–270 kg (375–595 lbs)
  • Lifespan: Up to 30 years
  • Diet: Fish, octopus, and crustaceans
  • Habitat: Warm subtropical waters, especially around the Hawaiian Islands and Mediterranean Sea
  • Behavior: Solitary or in small groups; rests on beaches or rocky shores
  • Reproduction: One pup per year, born on land or beaches
  • Conservation status: Endangered (IUCN Red List)
  • Population: Around 1,500 individuals (as of 2025)

Why is it endangered?

The monk seal is endangered due to a combination of historical hunting, habitat loss, entanglement in fishing gear, and human disturbance. The Mediterranean monk seal has suffered particularly steep declines, with populations fragmented and isolated. In Hawaii, the Hawaiian monk seal faces threats from limited food supply, marine debris, entanglement, and disease. Human activity on beaches can disrupt mothers and pups, leading to pup abandonment. Conservation efforts include protected areas, rehabilitation programs, and community education, but the species remains at risk and needs ongoing protection to recover.
